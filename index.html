Fighting inequality for a resilient future

This year's World Tsunami Awareness Day theme, "Fighting Inequality for a Resilient Future," raises awareness of the unequal risks that tsunamis pose, especially to vulnerable groups like women, children, the elderly, and people with disabilities. In the past century, tsunamis have claimed over 260,000 lives, leaving a deadly legacy despite their rarity. This startling statistic—which shows an average of 4,600 fatalities each disaster—beyonds the effects of any other natural hazard and emphasizes the need for proactive steps to reduce risks and improve community readiness.


Raising public awareness of the necessity of lowering tsunami dangers is the main goal of World Tsunami Awareness Day. The campaign, which is in line with the International Day for Disaster Reduction, highlights the necessity of group participation in disaster risk reduction initiatives from all societal sectors. The selected theme emphasizes how important it is to reduce injustice and develop tsunami resilience. In order to ensure a coordinated effort to lessen the effects of these uncommon but devastating natural disasters, the initiative seeks to build a more resilient and responsive foundation for communities through collaboration.
